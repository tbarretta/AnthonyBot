🚀 Getting Started

RetireSim is a personal retirement planning tool that lets you model different "what if" scenarios for your retirement — adjusting retirement age, spending, investment returns, Social Security claiming strategy, and more.

Unlike generic online calculators, RetireSim uses your actual account balances, income sources, and tax situation to produce a personalized projection. You can create multiple scenarios and compare them side by side.

⚡ Quick Start — 4 steps to your first simulation:

  1. Complete your Profile (age, income, life expectancy)
  2. Add your Investment Accounts (balances, contributions)
  3. Add your Income Sources (Social Security, pension, etc.)
  4. Create a Scenario and click Run Simulation

Workflow

RetireSim follows a layered data model. Your profile and accounts are shared across all scenarios; scenarios are where you change the variables.

1

Set up your Profile

Enter your current age, annual income, income growth rate, and expected life expectancy. If you have a spouse, add their details too. This data is the foundation every simulation is built on.

2

Add Investment Accounts

Enter each account — 401(k), IRA, Roth IRA, brokerage, HSA, etc. — with its current balance, annual contribution, employer match, and asset allocation. The simulation grows each account separately according to its tax type and allocation.

3

Add Income Sources

Add Social Security, pension, rental income, IUL distributions, part-time work, or any other guaranteed/recurring income. These reduce how much you need to withdraw from your portfolio in retirement.

4

Create Scenarios

A scenario is a set of assumptions: retirement age, spending target, return expectations, SS claiming age, tax rates, etc. Create multiple scenarios to compare outcomes — e.g. "Retire at 60 aggressive" vs "Retire at 67 conservative".

5

Run & Analyze

Run the simulation from the Scenarios page. Review the year-by-year projection, final balance, portfolio chart, and withdrawal breakdown. Adjust assumptions and re-run to see how changes affect the outcome.

Your Profile

Your profile holds the personal data that stays constant across all scenarios.

FieldWhat to enter
Current Age Your age today. The simulation starts here and runs year-by-year to your life expectancy.
Annual Income Your current gross employment income. Used to calculate contributions during the accumulation phase.
Income Growth Rate Expected annual salary increase %. Used to grow your income (and thus contribution capacity) each year before retirement.
Life Expectancy The age your plan runs to. Default is 90. Increase this to plan conservatively for a longer life.
Filing Status Used in Social Security taxation calculations (IRS combined income thresholds differ for single vs married filing jointly).
Spouse Add a spouse to include their income, retirement age, life expectancy, and SS claim age in the simulation. Spouse retirement age is set per-scenario.

Investment Accounts

Investment accounts are the growing assets that fund your retirement. The simulation grows each account separately during accumulation, then draws them down in tax-efficient order during retirement.

Withdrawal Order

The simulation withdraws from accounts in tax-efficient order: Taxable → Pre-tax → Roth. This minimises lifetime taxes by spending taxable gains first and letting tax-advantaged accounts compound longer.

Employer Match

Enter the match rate (% of your contribution the employer matches) and the match cap (% of salary). For example, "50% match up to 6% of salary" means if you contribute 6% of salary, the employer adds another 3%.

Asset Allocation

Enter the stock % and bond % for each account (must sum to 100%). The simulation applies the blended return based on your allocation and the scenario's expected stock/bond returns.

Income Sources

Income sources are guaranteed or recurring streams of income in retirement that reduce how much you need to withdraw from your portfolio. The more reliable income you have, the less portfolio risk you carry.

Social Security

Enter the monthly benefit at your Full Retirement Age (67) from your SSA statement at ssa.gov/myaccount. The benefit at other claim ages is calculated automatically using official SSA rules. The claim age is set per-scenario, so you can compare claiming at 62 vs 67 vs 70 across scenarios.

Pension

Enter the annual pension amount in today's dollars, the age it begins, and optionally a survivor benefit % for your spouse. Pensions can be marked as inflation-adjusted if they include a COLA.

Rental Income

Enter the expected net annual rental income, the age it starts (and optionally ends). Enable inflation adjustment if you expect rent to grow over time.

IUL / Annuity / Other

Use the same pattern: annual amount, start/end ages, inflation adjustment, and tax treatment. IUL distributions are typically tax-free — uncheck "This income is taxable" for those.

Note: Income sources are shared across all scenarios. The SS claim age is the one income-source variable that changes per scenario — everything else (the FRA benefit amount, COLA rate, pension amount, etc.) is fixed.

Scenarios

A scenario is a named set of assumptions for a single simulation run. You can create as many scenarios as you like and compare their outcomes. Use the Copy button on the Scenarios page to duplicate a scenario and tweak one variable at a time.

Key Scenario Parameters

ParameterDescription
Retirement Age The age at which you stop working and start drawing down your portfolio. Contributions stop; withdrawals begin.
Annual Spending (After Tax) The amount you want to spend each year in retirement, after taxes — what actually hits your bank account. The simulation automatically grosses up withdrawals to cover taxes before sending you this net amount.
Stock / Bond Returns Expected annual returns for stocks and bonds. Each account grows using its allocation-weighted blend of these rates. Historical long-run stock returns are ~7% real; bonds ~3.5%.
Inflation Rate Annual inflation %. Your spending target and inflation-adjusted income sources grow at this rate each year.
SS Claim Age The age at which you (and your spouse) claim Social Security. This is the primary lever — claiming at 70 vs 62 can mean a 77% higher monthly benefit for life.
Spending Strategy Fixed: inflation-adjusted spending every year. % of Portfolio: spend a fixed % of your portfolio value (the "4% rule"). Guardrails: reduce spending by 10% if your portfolio drops more than 20% below its peak.
Life Expectancy Override Override the profile-level life expectancy for this scenario. Useful for planning conservatively (e.g. plan to 95) while keeping your default profile at 90.
Tip: Use the Copy button on the Scenarios list to duplicate an existing scenario with "-copy" appended to the name. You'll land directly on the edit form — perfect for tweaking one variable at a time.

Running Simulations

Deterministic Simulation

The deterministic engine runs a single year-by-year projection using your fixed return rates. It's fast (<1 second) and gives you a clear baseline answer to "what happens if returns are exactly what I expect?"

How the Simulation Works

Accumulation phase (now → retirement): Each year, your income grows, contributions are added to accounts, employer match is applied, and accounts grow by their blended return rate.

Retirement phase (retirement → life expectancy): Employment income stops. Each year, the simulation calculates your inflation-adjusted spending target, subtracts guaranteed income (SS, pension, etc.), and withdraws the remaining gross amount from your portfolio in tax-efficient order.

RMDs: Required Minimum Distributions are automatically calculated and applied to pre-tax accounts starting at age 73 (per SECURE 2.0).

Depletion: If the portfolio runs out before your life expectancy age, the simulation flags the age at which it was depleted.

Re-running

You can re-run a scenario at any time from the Scenarios list. Each run creates a new result record; the most recent completed result is shown as the active result.

Reading Results

Final Balance

The portfolio value at your life expectancy age. Green = portfolio survived. Red = depleted, showing the age it ran out. A positive final balance means you have a buffer; a depleted result means you need to adjust assumptions.

Balance at Retirement

Your total portfolio value on the first day of retirement. This is the nest egg you're drawing from.

Portfolio Chart

The left axis shows portfolio balance over time. The right axis (dashed line) shows your annual after-tax spending target growing with inflation. The gap between spending and SS/pension income is what drives withdrawals.

Year-by-Year Table

Every year of the simulation is shown: your age, phase (accumulation / retirement), guaranteed income, after-tax spending target, gross portfolio withdrawal (grossed up for taxes), taxes paid on withdrawals, and year-end portfolio balance. RMD years are flagged.

Withdrawal vs Spending

Annual Spending is what you keep after taxes. Withdrawal Needed is the gross amount pulled from your portfolio before taxes — always larger when drawing from pre-tax accounts. The difference is Taxes Paid.

COLA (Cost of Living Adjustment)

Once you start collecting, your SS benefit grows each year by the COLA rate (default 2.5%). The simulation applies this compounding from your claim age forward.

Claim Age Strategy

The best claim age depends on your health, other income, and portfolio size. A key use of RetireSim is to create two identical scenarios — one claiming at 62, one at 70 — and compare the final balances. The answer often surprises people.

Taxes

Tax Rates in Scenarios

Effective Rate (Working): Applied to employment income during the accumulation phase for informational purposes.

Effective Rate (Retirement): The tax rate applied to pre-tax account withdrawals (401k, traditional IRA). This represents your blended effective federal + state rate on ordinary income in retirement.

Capital Gains Rate: Applied to approximately 50% of taxable brokerage withdrawals (the estimated gain portion).

After-Tax Spending & Gross-Up

The Annual Spending field is what you want to spend after taxes — your take-home amount. The simulation automatically computes how much more needs to come out of your portfolio to cover taxes:

Pre-tax 401k: need $80,000 net → withdraw $94,118 at 15% tax
Roth: need $80,000 net → withdraw $80,000 (tax-free)
Taxable: need $80,000 net → withdraw ~$86,486 at 15% cap gains (50% gain)

The gross-up uses the actual mix of account types being drawn from in tax-efficient order, so accounts are never over- or under-taxed.

Social Security Taxation

Up to 85% of SS benefits can be taxable depending on your combined income. The simulation uses IRS thresholds to determine the taxable portion of SS each year.

🌪️ Black Swan Events

Black swan events model sudden, severe market crashes — 2008, 2020, dot-com bust. Enabling this adds randomised crash events to your simulation so you can stress-test your plan against bad luck.

ParameterDescription
Annual Probability % The chance of a crash occurring in any given year. 3% means roughly one event per 33 years on average.
Min / Max Loss % The range of portfolio loss when a crash hits. A loss is drawn randomly from this range. Default 20–50%.
Recovery Years How many years the portfolio's return rate is suppressed after the crash as it gradually recovers. Default 3 years.
Black swan events are applied in the deterministic simulation using a seeded random process. If you re-run the same scenario, the crash timing may differ. Use this feature to explore worst-case planning, not as a statistical model.

Invitations

RetireSim is invite-only. New users can only register using a valid invitation link — there is no open sign-up. This keeps the user base controlled and the application personal.

For Administrators

Staff users can create invitation links from the Invitations page in the navigation bar. Each invitation link is single-use — once a user registers with it, the link is consumed. You can generate as many as needed and share them via email or message.

For New Users

If you've been given an invitation link:

  1. Click the invitation link
  2. Create a username and password on the registration page
  3. You'll be taken directly to the profile setup wizard
  4. Complete your profile and start building your plan
Invitation links expire after 7 days. If your link has expired, ask the administrator to generate a new one.
